To let someone go is to put their wants and needs above your own. To hold on to someone who no longer wants to be with you is self-serving.

In the past years, I have been trying, holding on to you, and I always thought that what I was doing was for both of us—that one day we would find love and you would find me. I kept waiting for you in silence.

But if someone is questioning whether they want to leave my life, the strongest thing I can do is to show them the door. I love you, but you are free to leave if you choose to. I must support your best interests and your decisions. Letting you go when you want to leave is an act of support. The final act of support is to truly stay away from you. I must respect your decision and support you, even if that means you are no longer in my life. Even though it hurts me badly, even though my heart is full of you and you alone, this is the last thing I can do for you.

I am strong enough to love you from afar, to stay away from you as you live out your life without me. Because I love you, I let you go. I give you support as you leave my life, and my final act of support is maintaining the strength to not reach out to you. Even though staying away causes me grief, I am proud that I hold true to my word and protect your happiness.

Let you go and One day, there will be a person who walks into my life and shuts the door behind them.
